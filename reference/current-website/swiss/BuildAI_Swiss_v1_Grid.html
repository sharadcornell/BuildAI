01The Goal

Close the gap between
graduating and shipping.

Indian engineering colleges produce strong theory. AI-first startups hire for something else: shipping velocity, engineering judgment, and fluency with AI-assisted workflows. BuildAI exists to close that gap — in 13 weeks, inside the college, with real products as the only proof.

  • NOTan AI course — students build, they don't watch
  • NOTa bootcamp — it runs alongside the semester
  • NOTa certification mill — ~30% earn Apprentice Certified or above
  • ISan apprenticeship: real products, reviewed weekly by working engineers, defended publicly

02The Programme

A week inside BuildAI is a week
inside an AI startup.

Async on weekdays, live with mentors on Saturday, peer review on Sunday. 15–20 hours a week. Students ship from week one.

03Curriculum

Six modules, organised around
what startups hire for.

M0Week 1

AI-Native Operating System

Cursor, Claude Code, context engineering. Ship one app by Friday of week one.

M1Weeks 2–4

Product Engineering

Next.js, Postgres, auth, deploys, monitoring. Two live apps with real users.

M2Weeks 5–7

AI Product Patterns

LLM APIs, RAG, agents. One real AI-powered product in production.

M3Week 8

Evaluation & Reliability

Eval-driven development. A regression suite that actually catches failures.

M4Weeks 9–10

Shipping in a Startup

Teams of 3–4. Linear, PRs, sprint rituals, production code review.

M5Weeks 11–13

Capstone

Public launch with real users — reviewed by an external AI-startup engineer.

04Certification

Earned, not granted.

Seven dimensions, scored 1–5 by two internal reviewers — and validated externally by working startup engineers at week 13.

~70%
Participated

Finished the programme, completed all assignments.

~20%
Apprentice Certified

All seven rubric dimensions scored 3 or higher.

~10%
Distinction

Externally validated. Fast-tracked to startup placement.

No job guarantee — by design. The certificate keeps its value because it cannot be bought. Scarcity is what creates startup trust.
