LEAD STORY

We don't teach AI. We make engineers who ship.

Inside the apprenticeship that grades India's engineering students the way startups do — on deployed products, not exam papers.

BY SHARAD AGRAWAL — FOUNDER, BUILDAI · IIT BOMBAY '18 · CORNELL MBA '24

Every Indian engineering college teaches theory. Almost none teach what AI-first startups actually hire for: shipping velocity, engineering judgment, and fluency with AI-assisted workflows. The gap is structural — and BuildAI's bet is that it is closeable in thirteen weeks, inside the college, alongside the semester.

Students work in pods of eight to twelve under a working engineer from an AI startup. They ship from week one. Every Friday ends in a recorded demo; every Saturday in a live architecture review. By week thirteen, each student defends a publicly launched product before an external reviewer — an engineer who hires for a living.

01 — DISPATCH · THE GOAL

Graduate builders, not certificate holders.

What it is not

Not an AI course — nobody watches lecture videos. Not a bootcamp — no career break, it runs alongside the semester. Not a certification mill — roughly 30% of each cohort earns Apprentice Certified or above, by design.

What it is

An apprenticeship: real products, reviewed weekly by working engineers from AI startups, defended publicly at the end. The certificate is scarce because scarcity is what makes startups trust it.

Why colleges sign

A credible AI-native programme for NAAC, AICTE and admissions; public portfolios and demo archives the placement cell can put in front of any recruiter; zero faculty burden — BuildAI runs delivery end-to-end; and an administrative margin of ₹5,000–₹7,000 per enrolled student.
