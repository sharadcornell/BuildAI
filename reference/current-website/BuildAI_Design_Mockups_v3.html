Abstract. Disclosed herein is a system and method for the practical formation of applied AI product engineers, comprising: (a) a remote-first delivery layer integrated with engineering colleges in tier-2 Indian cities; (b) pods of eight to twelve apprentices, each supervised by a working engineer drawn from contemporary AI startups; (c) a thirteen-week build cycle terminating in a publicly reviewed capstone; and (d) a graduated certification scheme. No lectures are employed. The system is characterised by its substitution of shipping velocity for examination, and of working code for slide-based instruction.

Original Article · The Programme

Anatomy of a thirteen-week
applied AI apprenticeship.

The architecture of the BuildAI programme is built on three premises that, in combination, define its outputs and economics. First, apprentices are placed into pods of eight to twelve, each pod under a single working engineer drawn from a contemporary AI-first startup. Second, all teaching is delivered through the act of shipping — no lectures are administered. Third, the cohort term is fixed at thirteen weeks, aligned to existing intensive windows in the Indian engineering college calendar.
