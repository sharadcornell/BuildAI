OPTION E

Indian editorial — long-form magazine feature

The visual language of The Caravan, Open, or an India Today long-form feature. Cream paper, Cormorant serif, two-column body, drop caps, sepia photography, vermilion accent, byline/dateline, page folio. Premium, distinctly Indian, refuses generic global tech. Reads as if a serious journalist wrote about your program — the deck IS the feature.

The Program · Section 04

A thirteen-week apprenticeship for engineers who'd rather ship than sit through slides.

A new kind of programme is being built for India's tier-2 engineering colleges — one that treats undergraduates the way an AI startup treats its first three hires.
Words — BuildAI / Photographs — Demo Day archive
For every hundred undergraduates in a tier-2 CS programme, perhaps twenty have the instincts of a builder. They write code that wasn't assigned. They deploy on weekends. They watch what their seniors did at startups and want that, not the campus placement queue. BuildAI exists for them — and for the colleges that quietly already know who they are.

OPTION F

Risograph zine / two-colour overprint

Halftone dot texture, two-ink overprint (fluoro pink + cyan, registration deliberately off by a few pixels), heavy block-letter headlines, monospaced body. References Riso-printed zines, art-school posters, indie-press culture. Confident and modern; deliberately imperfect. Best for student-facing materials and engineer-recruitment one-pagers — likely too irreverent for TPO sales meetings.

OPTION G

Swiss modernist / international typographic style

The visual language of Müller-Brockmann, Vignelli, IBM's 1962–1980s identity. Mathematical 12-column grid, primary-colour blocks as compositional elements, asymmetric layout, condensed sans-serif, severe horizontals. Cold, intellectual, "we read Vignelli." Distinct from the warm/earthy options — this is the precise/architectural variant.
